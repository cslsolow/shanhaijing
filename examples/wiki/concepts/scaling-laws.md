# Scaling Laws

Empirical power-law relationships between neural network performance and key factors: model size (parameters), dataset size (tokens), and compute budget (FLOPs). For Transformer language models, loss decreases predictably as each factor increases, enabling reliable extrapolation of training outcomes.

Scaling laws transformed AI research from trial-and-error experimentation to predictive engineering: teams can estimate final model performance from small-scale experiments, plan compute allocation, and make informed cost-benefit decisions before committing to expensive training runs.
